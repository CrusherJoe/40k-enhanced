# Boogeyman archetypes — Necrons (two "un-killable" poles)

Two GT-era Necron lists. Stats ingested live from BSData (`Xenos - Necrons.cat`
rev106). Necrons add a THIRD axis beyond melta↔flamer and durable↔horde:
**Reanimation (chip is refunded → must kill outright)** and **invuln/monster
resilience that partially negates "AP is king."** The practical upshot: against
top-end Necrons, **you play the mission, you don't table them.**

## List 1 — Obeisance Phalanx (Disruption): Monolith spam
| Unit | Stats | Threat weapon |
|---|---|---|
| **3× Monolith** (420–440 ea) | **T13 W22 Sv2+ OC8** | Death ray S12 **AP-4** D6+1; Particle whip Blast Dev Wounds; Portal (teleport) |
| The Silent King | T10 W16 2+/**4++** OC6 | 2× Annihilator beam S14 **AP-4** D6 |
| Lokhust Heavy Destroyers | T6 W4 3+ | Gauss destructor **S14 AP-4 D6**, Lethal Hits — sniper anti-tank |
| Imotekh, Canoptek Spyder, Ophydians | support / DS melee | reanimation buffs + board control |
| *Reanimation Protocols* | — | each unit reanimates **D3 wounds** every Command phase |

**Threat profile.** *Durability:* absurd and concentrated — **1300 pts of T13/W22/2+
pyramids** with OC8 apiece. My melta is S8–9: **it wounds T13 on 5+**. Even a full melta
alpha struggles to drop one Monolith, and it reanimates D3/turn. → **These are effectively
un-killable for Sisters; don't try.** They're slow (M8") — out-maneuver them and score
around them. *What kills my girls:* low-volume, high-quality **AP-4 anti-tank** (12 death
rays + annihilators + gauss destructors) that deletes my **Immolators/Paragons** and
overkills infantry — so transports are exposed to *this*, not to volume. *Objective game:*
Disruption + OC8 immovable objects; I win the *rest* of the board with speed.

**Threat profile.** *Durability:* 4× **T11 W16** C'tan with invulns + damage-mitigation.
**Here "AP is king" partly breaks:** vs a 4++ invuln, AP-4 melta is no better than AP-2,
and S9 melta wounds T11 on 5+. **You cannot table 4 C'tan.** → **Don't try** — kill the
*killable* enablers (Lokhust, Lychguard via volume, Reanimator, Plasmancer, characters)
and race the mission. *Danger:* it's **Purge the Foe** — the C'tan farm VP off my dead
units, so I must **feed them as little as possible** while scoring faster. *What kills my
girls:* devastating-quality melee/shooting (S14 AP-4 Dev-Wounds scythe kills anything;
Gaze/Voltaic/Seismic) — moderate volume, but each hit overkills.

## THE NECRON LESSON — mission-focus, and don't over-melta the un-killable
1. **Reanimation → chip is refunded.** Only *outright* kills count — favours concentrated
   high-damage removing whole models/units in one activation, and makes killing tough
   Necron units doubly hard.
2. **Invulns + T11/T13 + C'tan mitigation partially negate AP.** Against a 4++ or T13,
   melta's AP-4 edge evaporates and raw wounding falls off (S9 → wounds on 5s).
3. **⇒ The correct plan vs top-end Necrons is MISSION, not kill.** Sisters can't reliably
   table 3 Monoliths or 4 C'tan. Win on the *objective game*: speed, OC bodies,
   secondaries, Acts-of-Faith reliability on key rolls, deny their actions, and spend
   melta only to finish damaged targets / kill the killable support that enables them.

**This is a real correction to "AP is king, melta everything":** AP rules vs Marines and
most Orks, but vs invuln-heavy Necron monsters, **tabling is off the table — so don't
over-invest melta chasing un-killable targets.** A *moderate* melta package + strong
mobility/OC/scoring beats an all-in killer. It reinforces the emerging thesis:

> **Sisters' real edge across this whole meta is the MISSION game — cheap OC, jump speed,
> Miracle-dice clutch, being everywhere — not out-slugging. Build a balanced
> melta+flamer+blast toolkit on a transport/mobility chassis, and win on points.**

**Detachment read (post-4-factions):** *Champions of Faith* (+1 Move / +1 WS-BS / +1 Ld
to 3 Battle Sisters/Sacresant/Paragon units, Disruption disposition) is climbing — it
buys **mobility for the mission game AND accuracy for the melta** at once. *Hallowed
Martyrs* stays strong (flexible, rewards inevitable casualties). *Bringers of Flame* is
out for all-comers.
