# Stress-test — Knights List C (Horde-Hardened) vs the Green Tide

Turn-by-turn, disposition LOCKED Purge the Foe. Vs the Green Tide's Take-and-Hold
I play **Unstoppable Force** (3/kill · 4/objective · 5/central · 3/newly-taken).
All numbers from the mathhammer (`docs/knights-matchup-plans.md` + the sim run).

## The forces
- **Me (C):** Valiant (DOMINUS; Conflagration + S24 harpoon + twin meltagun), Warden
  (Avenger + Heavy Flamer + Icarus), Crusader (Avenger + thermal + RFBC), 3× Warglaive
  (thermal spear + reaper), 2× Helverin (autocannon), Navigator (home).
- **Them:** ~100 Boyz (5×20, T5 W1 5+, choppa + power-klaw nobs), **3 Painboys → FNP 5+**,
  Ghazghkull + Makari, 2 Warboss + 3 Bigboss, 10 Kommandos (infiltrate), Stormboyz (jump),
  Gretchin, Wazdakka. ~135 bodies.

## The three facts that decide it
1. **I kill ~24 Boyz/turn into FNP mobs** (~36 without), **+~5 on flamer Overwatch** when charged.
2. **Super-Heavy Walker:** big Knights Fall Back and STILL shoot → **the horde cannot tarpit them.**
   Armigers can't — they're the screen you trade.
3. **OC is unwinnable straight-up** (100 Boyz OC2 = 200 vs my ~60). So I do NOT race objectives —
   I **kill Boyz OFF contested objectives** (my OC10 then wins them) and **hold what they can't reach**.
   Unstoppable Force's kill clause (3/turn) is free — I destroy a mob most turns.

## Turn by turn (I take first turn — see risks)
**R1 · Me:** advance big Knights to firing lanes (centre terrain blocks T1 LOS); long guns
(2× Avenger 36", 2× Helverin 48", RFBC 72") into the lead mobs + Kommandos → **~17 Boyz**. A
Knight sits on central C1 (OC10). Navigator locks home. *VP ≈ 3.*
**R1 · Orks:** hold the Waaagh; advance everything; Kommandos/Stormboyz grab C2 + expansion E1.
*VP ≈ 0.*

**R2 · Me (peak):** flamers now in range. **Harpoon + twin meltagun kill Ghazghkull** (~9-12).
Conflagration + 2× Avenger + 3× reaper sweep + RFBC → **~26 Boyz** (FNP-adjusted). Boyz ≈ 55.
Hold C1 (central); Warglaive contests E1. *VP ≈ 15 (me ~18).*
**R2 · Orks (WAAAGH!):** advance-and-charge. **Overwatch flamers auto-hit ~5.** A 20-mob + klaw
(~17 on Waaagh) kills a Warglaive — but it **tarpits that mob**. A mob hits the Valiant: ~11-16,
it shrugs (28W). They flood C2/E1 + contest C1. *Determined Acquisition ≈ 12 (them ~12).*

**R3 · Me:** **Fall Back the engaged Valiant and STILL shoot** (Super-Heavy Walker) — not tarpitted.
Clear the Boyz on C1/E1 (~24). Boyz ≈ 28. Cleared objectives flip to my OC10. *VP ≈ 13 (me ~31).*
**R3 · Orks:** bleeding; fewer mobs reach; Overwatch + attrition. Hold their home + one central.
*VP ≈ 8 (them ~20).*

**R4 · Me:** ~20 more → Boyz ≈ 8 + stragglers. Control C1+C2+E1. *VP ≈ 15 (me ~46).*
**R4 · Orks:** broken remnants. *VP ≈ 5 (them ~25).*

**R5 · Me:** mop up; hold centre + expansion, push their home. *VP ≈ 12 (me ~58).*
**R5 · Orks:** remnants. *VP ≈ 4 (them ~29).*

## Verdict
**C wins — roughly 58–31 on primary** (plus comparable secondaries) — **but it's a disciplined
grind, not a blowout.** Their primary peaks R2-3 when they flood objectives, then craters as I
clear the Boyz faster than they can hold. My kill-clause + central-hold ramp as theirs falls.

## It swings on FOUR things (get these wrong and it flips)
1. **FIRST TURN is the biggest lever.** If the Orks go first + Waaagh T1, I lose a shooting phase
   and eat the charge before thinning them — their early primary spikes and it becomes a coin-flip.
   Bid/deploy for first turn; if you're going second, deploy deeper and lean harder on Overwatch.
2. **Screen discipline.** Armigers MUST body-block the big Knights (trade Warglaives to tarpit mobs).
   Let two mobs reach a big Knight and even a Valiant can be ground down over two turns.
3. **Kill Ghazghkull R2** (harpoon) — removes their melee anchor and de-fangs the Waaagh.
4. **Fall Back + shoot every time** (Super-Heavy Walker). Never sit still letting the swarm hold you;
   reposition, re-target, keep the kill-rate up.

## Does the sim change the list?
**No — it validates C as the right list; the edge is in the PLAN, not the roster.** The anti-horde
(~24/turn into FNP) is *just* enough — a grind by design. The only marginal note: the 2 Helverins
are anti-elite (weak vs Boyz) — they earn their slot killing Kommandos/characters + range + bodies,
so keep them, but know they're not part of the Boyz-clearing math. **Don't chase the OC race; kill,
screen, fall-back-shoot, and let Unstoppable Force's kill clause carry you.**
