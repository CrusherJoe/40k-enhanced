# Turn-by-turn plan — Lancer list, Purge the Foe

The list (examples/threat-saturation-purge.yaml, 1995/2000):
Castellan (**Archeotech Autoloaders**) · Castellan (**Blessed Plate**, T13) ·
Crusader (**RFBC + Judicant's Helm**) · **Cerastus Lancer** · Armiger Warglaive ·
**Navigator** (Agents ally, home anchor).

## How this list wins Purge the Foe

Every one of your five missions pays for the **same two things**: **killing units**
(3–5 VP most turns) and **holding objectives excluding your home** (4 VP/turn from
round 2), with a late bonus for taking the **opponent's home / central** (5–8 VP).
You do both natively: the guns kill, and your big Knights **hold with OC 10 while
they shoot** (56–80" range — they don't have to choose). The Navigator locks your
home so you bank that VP for free and **commit everything else forward**. You win
by out-killing on the board while parking Knights on the mid-board objectives.

## The pieces and their jobs

- **2 Castellans** — the engine. Whole-board range; their job is to delete the
  enemy's key units and sit on central/expansion objectives (OC 10) while doing it.
  One re-rolls its casino dice (Archeotech), one is T13 (Blessed Plate).
- **Crusader (RFBC + Judicant)** — flexible gun + the cover key. Each Shooting
  phase, **Judicant gives one Castellan [IGNORES COVER]** (must be within 12" +
  LoS — keep it near the gunline). RFBC is your anti-horde/anti-elite flex.
- **Lancer** — the distraction + flank breaker. 14" move, S20 lance, **4+ invuln
  vs everything**. It demands a response; if ignored, it charges and deletes a key
  unit or walks onto the enemy's home objective late. Its **Bondsman gives the
  Warglaive advance-and-charge.**
- **Warglaive** — fast grabber/finisher; rides the Lancer's Bondsman to advance +
  charge; thermal spear kills a tank/monster.
- **Navigator** — sits on your home objective **in dense terrain**, **never
  shoots** (to stay Hidden + Gone to Ground → detection range 12"), and its Gaze
  blocks enemy Deep Strike/ingress within 12". Near-unremovable home VP.

## Free mobility — the Valourstrike rule

**Advance any one unit and every Knight's ranged weapons gain [ASSAULT] for the
turn.** So you never choose move-or-shoot: advance out of hiding, into a lane, or
onto an objective, and still fire the full salvo. This is the backbone of every
turn — treat your whole gunline as mobile.

## Cover — your standing problem, and the four answers

Cover is a −1 to hit and it's the **default** (16 obscuring terrain areas). You
mitigate it four ways, in priority order per shot:
1. **Judicant** → the Castellan it feeds ignores cover entirely (best on your
   highest-value target that turn).
2. **Dominus (+1 vs targets in a terrain area)** → cancels cover for a Castellan
   shooting something *standing in* terrain.
3. **Plunging Fire** → a Castellan (TOWERING) shooting a **ground-level** target
   **within 12"** gets +1 BS (offsets cover) — situational, close range only.
4. **Positioning** → angle for lanes with no intervening terrain; the Castellans'
   whole-board range means you're picking *angles*, not closing distance.

# Pre-game and deployment (universal)

1. **Muster / roster:** lock **Purge the Foe** as your disposition.
2. **Navigator first, on your home objective**, tucked into/behind a **dense**
   terrain feature so it's Hidden. If the home objective has no dense terrain,
   put it behind the nearest dense piece within range (3") of the marker. It must
   never fire.
3. **Hide the gunline turn 1.** Deploy the Castellans + Crusader behind terrain
   (Towering, so use the tallest pieces / dense terrain to block LoS). You are a
   low-model army — do **not** give a turn-1 alpha strike clean shots, especially
   if you go second.
4. **Lancer on the flank you intend to pressure**, positioned to advance up a side
   lane. Warglaive next to it (within Bondsman range) so it inherits advance-and-charge.
5. **First turn:** if you get it, deploy a touch more forward and take early lanes;
   if you're going second, prioritise hiding — you'll emerge turn 1 with Assault.

# The turn-by-turn arc (the skeleton every game follows)

**Turn 1 — Emerge and chip.** Advance the gunline out of hiding into firing lanes
(Assault turns on the moment anything advances). Kill the **most dangerous enemy
unit you can see** (bank the 3 VP for "a unit was destroyed"). Push the Lancer up
its flank ~14"+advance, staying just out of a bad counter-charge; it starts
drawing attention. Warglaive moves with it. Navigator stays home, silent.
*Don't overcommit — round 1 is positioning + first blood.*

**Turn 2 — The kill-and-hold turn.** This is when primary scoring opens ("Second
Battle Round Onwards"). Move Knights onto **central objectives** (OC 10 holds them)
and fire. Feed Judicant to the Castellan with the juiciest cover-denied target.
Lancer charges its first victim (S20, +1 to wound on the charge) or bodies onto an
objective; Warglaive advance-charges a tank/backfield unit. **Score: kill (3) +
hold ≥1 non-home objective (4).**

**Turn 3 — Press the advantage / the swing turn.** Keep deleting priority targets
(Meatgrinder & Destroyer's Wrath pay 4–5 VP for "killed more than they did last
turn" — so trade up). Extend to a second/central objective. The Lancer is now deep
— aim it at the **enemy's home objective** for the late-game bonus. Manage the
cover stack every shooting phase.

**Turns 4–5 — Convert and close.** Lock the objectives you hold, finish wounded
units for the kill VP, and (per mission) **take the opponent's home / hold central**
for the big end-of-battle points (5–8 VP). If a Knight is dying, remember **Deadly
Demise** — position it so its detonation punishes clustered enemies. Navigator has
held home all game.

# Deployment-geometry playbook (A/B/C rotate through these)

- **Horizontal / Dawn of War (~18" zones, ~8" No Man's Land — close):** you can
  pressure turn 1–2. Hide behind the mid-line terrain, emerge and shoot early. The
  Lancer reaches combat by turn 2. Watch for enemy alpha strikes — the short gap
  cuts both ways.
- **Vertical / Hammer-and-Anvil (~12–14" zones, wide No Man's Land — far):** long
  approach. Lean on the gunline (you out-range everything) and use Assault's extra
  reach to walk up while shooting; the Lancer needs ~2 turns to connect, so use it
  to threaten a flank objective rather than an early charge. More time to gunline =
  good for you.
- **Diagonal / Crucible / Tipping-point (corner-to-corner):** castle your gunline
  in a corner with good sightlines; the center terrain cluster blocks cross-board
  LoS, so take an early lane on one diagonal. Run the Lancer up the long diagonal
  at the enemy's deployment corner. Navigator anchors your corner home.

# The five matchups (your mission vs theirs)

**vs Take and Hold → you play Unstoppable Force / they play Immovable Object.**
They want to sit and hold everything. Your edge: you out-shoot a static army and
Purge pays you for killing. Priority: kill their holders off the mid-board
objectives, then park Knights on them. **End-of-battle bonus: control a central
objective (5)** — make sure a Knight is sitting central turn 5.

**vs Purge the Foe → Meatgrinder (mirror).** A kill-race. You win it: more,
tougher, longer-ranged guns + the Lancer. **Trade up every turn** ("more enemy
units destroyed this turn than friendly last turn" = 5 VP). Late, drive the Lancer
onto **their home objective (5 VP)**. Protect your own Knights' positioning — don't
feed them into their return fire.

**vs Disruption → Punishment / they play Delaying Action.** *Special:* at the start
of each turn you **condemn 1–3 enemy units** (near objectives or that killed your
stuff); you score **5 VP whenever a condemned unit leaves the battlefield** — i.e.
when you kill it. This mission is tailor-made for your guns: **condemn the units
you were going to shoot anyway, then delete them.** Hold ≥1 objective (4) + more
objectives than them (5); late, take **their home (8 VP)** with the Lancer/Warglaive.

**vs Reconnaissance → Consecrate / they play Triangulation.** *Special:* each unit
that **destroys** an enemy becomes a "consecration unit"; at end of turn it
**consecrates an objective it's within range of** (place a marker). So **kill
enemies while your Knights are on/near objectives** → those objectives consecrate
themselves. 3 VP (1–2 consecrated) / 6 VP (3+). Push a killer onto **their home to
consecrate it late (5 VP)** — the Lancer or a Warglaive that just made a kill.
Recon armies are fast/elusive — use the Lancer to hunt their scoring pieces.

**vs Priority Assets → Destroyer's Wrath / they play Vital Link.** They lean on
Objective Actions and operation markers. You don't care about their actions —
**kill their action-doers** (3 VP + the "more than last turn" 4 VP) and **out-hold
them (6 VP for more objectives than the opponent)**. Your OC-10 Knights win the
objective count; their fragile action units die to your guns.

# Decision rules / drills

- **Every Shooting phase, resolve the cover stack first:** pick the Judicant
  recipient (best cover-denied target), point the Dominus Castellans at in-terrain
  targets, and note any sub-12" ground targets for Plunging Fire.
- **Target priority:** kill the thing that (a) most threatens your Knights or (b)
  scores you the mission (condemned units in Punishment; units near objectives in
  Consecrate). Purge always pays for the kill regardless.
- **Lancer usage:** it's a threat first, a hammer second. Position it so the
  opponent must either divert fire (good — your gunline shoots free) or eat a
  charge (good — S20). Don't throw it into a screen it can't break; angle it at
  soft, high-value targets and the enemy home objective.
- **Never shoot the Navigator's gun.** Ever. It breaks Hidden/Gone to Ground and
  opens it to sniping.
- **Hold with Knights, not hope:** a Castellan on an objective holds it (OC 10)
  and shoots — default to parking a Knight on each central objective you can reach.
- **Watch Deadly Demise:** a dying Knight is a bomb; if one's going down, face it
  into clustered enemies.
